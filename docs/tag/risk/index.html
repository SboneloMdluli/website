Understanding Bond Interest Rate Risk

A bond’s price sensitivity to interest rate changes is a crucial concept in fixed income markets. This analysis explores how duration and convexity help measure and understand this relationship.

Price Sensitivity

The price of a bond is inversely proportional to its yield to maturity. For a 25-year 6% coupon bond with a yield of 9% and face value of $100:

  • A bond’s duration measures its price sensitivity to small interest rate changes
  • Higher duration means greater interest rate risk
  • Longer-term bonds typically have higher duration
  • Convexity helps improve price change estimates for larger rate movements

For example, a 2% change in interest rates would cause approximately a 17% change in the bond’s price when accounting for both duration and convexity effects.
